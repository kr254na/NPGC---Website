About Department

Department at a Glance

The Department of Psychology was established in 1979 with undergraduate programs, marking the beginning of its academic journey. After 27 years of sustained commitment to excellence in teaching and learning, the department expanded its academic offerings by introducing a Master’s degree program, with a strong emphasis on practical training and research orientation. Currently, the department offers three-year and four-year Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) along with one year and two year Master of Arts (M.A.) in Psychology. The curriculum is thoughtfully designed to integrate theoretical understanding with practical application, ensuring the holistic development of students. The department strives to create an engaging and academically enriching environment. It is equipped with a dedicated departmental library housing more than 350 books, along with a well-equipped laboratory featuring over 112 modern psychological tools and instruments to facilitate experiential learning. The department also operates an active counseling cell that provides free counseling and psycho-education services. This initiative supports not only students from all streams but also faculty members, contributing to overall mental well-being and awareness within the institution.
